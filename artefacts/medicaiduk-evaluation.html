Evaluation of MedicaidUK Final Project vs. Initial Proposal

Overview

This artefact evaluates the group project and final implementation of the MedicaidUK database project (Unit 11) against the initial proposal (Unit 6), analysing developments in design, compliance, and scalability.

Evaluation

The initial project proposal (Unit 6) for MedicaidUK established a strong logical foundation, clearly identifying entities, relationships, and compliance requirements. The proposed relational MySQL design focused on structured data integrity, GDPR alignment, and scalability. It prioritised clinical efficiency through role-based access, validation pipelines, and secure transaction handling. By contrast, the final project (Unit 11) demonstrates a matured understanding of healthcare data governance and operational optimisation. The executive summary extends the proposal’s technical outline into a fully realised data management framework, incorporating enhanced data validation, audit trails, and multi-stage cleansing pipelines (Guo et al., 2023). The integration of compliance frameworks (ICO, 2023; NHS Digital, 2024) and role-based security (Cozzoli et al., 2022) shows the project’s evolution from concept to robust, regulation-compliant practice. While the proposal emphasised design logic and entity relationships, the final version critically evaluates system scalability, performance trade-offs, and NoSQL interoperability—acknowledging relational limits for unstructured clinical data. This reflective comparison highlights improved data governance, ethical awareness, and future adaptability through hybrid integration. Overall, the final project advances from theoretical design to a comprehensive, auditable, and ethically resilient healthcare database, meeting both operational and compliance objectives initially envisioned.

Reflection

This comparison reinforced my understanding of database lifecycle management—from conceptual design to secure implementation. The process highlighted the importance of iterative review, stakeholder validation, and regulatory alignment in healthcare data systems.
